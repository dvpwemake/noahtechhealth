5. Regulatory Framework

This study and its recruitment materials are designed to comply with applicable human subjects protection requirements, including:

  • 21 CFR Part 50 — Protection of Human Subjects (informed consent)
  • 21 CFR Part 56 — Institutional Review Boards
  • 21 CFR Part 312 — Investigational New Drug Application (stem cell therapy arm)
  • 21 CFR Part 812 — Investigational Device Exemptions (wearable BCI arm)
  • ICH E6(R2/R3) GCP — Good Clinical Practice for recruitment material approval and version control
  • HIPAA Privacy Rule — Protected health information safeguards at enrollment

6. Pre-Screening vs. Enrollment

Noah Tech distinguishes between pre-screening interest and formal study enrollment:

  • Pre-screening interest — Submitting the Trial Pre-Screening Interest Form authorizes the study team to contact you about potential eligibility. It is not informed consent and does not enroll you in the study.
  • Eligibility screening — Conducted under protocol procedures after recruitment opens and only after appropriate authorizations are in place.
  • Enrollment — Requires a signed IRB-approved Informed Consent Form and, where applicable, a HIPAA Authorization. Enrollment is voluntary and may be withdrawn at any time.

7. Participant Rights

Prospective and enrolled participants have the right to:

  • Receive accurate, non-misleading information about the study before deciding to participate
  • Ask questions of the study team or IRB of record at any time
  • Decline participation or withdraw without penalty to clinical care outside the study
  • Report concerns about study conduct to the IRB of record (contact details to be posted upon approval)
  • Request information about how personal data is collected and used per our Privacy Policy
