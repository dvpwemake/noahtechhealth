Section 4: Clinical Trial Workflow for NoahTech MCI Suite SaMD – Time, Cost Estimates & Funding Resources (Current April 2026)

Key Definition: Pivotal RCT (Pivotal Randomized Controlled Trial)
A Pivotal RCT is the definitive, large-scale confirmatory clinical study that provides the primary evidence of safety and effectiveness required by the FDA for marketing authorization (510(k) or De Novo). It is the “make-or-break” trial that regulators rely on for approval decisions. Unlike early pilot/feasibility studies (which only need to show “reasonable expectation” of benefit for Breakthrough designation), a Pivotal RCT must be prospectively designed, statistically powered, and typically randomized against a control (sham/standard care) with clear primary endpoints (e.g., statistically significant improvement in MoCA or ADAS-Cog scores).

Current FDA Policy (April 2026): The 2013 guidance “Design Considerations for Pivotal Clinical Investigations for Medical Devices” remains fully active. Breakthrough designation allows sponsors to negotiate the exact Pivotal RCT design with FDA via sprint discussions and a Clinical Protocol Agreement, significantly reducing regulatory risk.
