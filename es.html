The MCI & Dementia Landscape

720,000 Californians living with Alzheimer's—the highest absolute volume in the United States.
92% Of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) cases across the nation go completely undetected.
13.8M Projected U.S. dementia cases by 2060 without population-scale screening protocols.
10%–15% Annual progression rate from undetected MCI to irreversible dementia.
  • The Hidden Target: An estimated 1.2M+ Californians currently live with undetected MCI. This is the critical transitional phase and the only window where cognitive decline is clinically reversible.
  • The Caregiver Toll: 40% of family caregivers suffer from clinical depression, and 1 in 8 caregivers over the age of 45 report experiencing subjective cognitive decline themselves.

The Critical Paradox: Science vs. The System

The Science Exists

  • Reversible Cognitive Decline: Up to 51% of localized MCI cases can successfully revert to normal cognitive function with early, structured multidomain intervention.
  • Preventable Risk Factors: The Lancet Commission confirms that 45% of global dementia cases are entirely preventable by managing 14 modifiable risk factors.

The System Fails Us

  • Severe Diagnostic Barriers: Traditional clinical neuropsychological evaluations suffer from extreme specialist shortages, causing weeks of administrative wait time.
  • The Missed Window: Primary care settings currently catch fewer than 10% of expected MCI cases, allowing the reversible window to close unused.
